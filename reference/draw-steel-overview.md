# Draw Steel -- High-Level Overview

Draw Steel is a heroic fantasy TTRPG by MCDM Productions. It is a tactical, cinematic game about extraordinary heroes fighting monsters and villains. It is not a dungeon crawler, hex crawl, survival game, or horror game -- it is specifically about strategic combat, teamwork, and dramatic storytelling.

## Core Design Philosophy

- **Tactical**: Played on a grid. Positioning, teamwork, and combos matter. Players plan together.
- **Heroic**: Heroes are assumed to be good. No ammo tracking, encumbrance, rations, or torches. Only "on-screen" stuff matters.
- **Cinematic**: Abilities are evocative and visually describable. Fights feel like action movie scenes.
- **Always progressing**: Abilities always deal damage (no "I miss, next turn"). Fights typically last 3 rounds or fewer.

## The Power Roll

All rolls use 2d10 + characteristic. Three tier outcomes:

| Tier | Roll |
|------|------|
| Tier 1 | 11 or lower |
| Tier 2 | 12-16 |
| Tier 3 | 17+ |

Natural 19-20 always achieves tier 3 and is a critical hit on ability rolls.

Modifiers: Edges (+2), Banes (-2). Double edge/bane shifts the tier result up/down by one instead of adding a flat bonus.

## Characteristics

Five stats, each ranging from -5 to +5:

- **Might** -- Strength, brawn
- **Agility** -- Coordination, nimbleness
- **Reason** -- Logic, education
- **Intuition** -- Instincts, experience
- **Presence** -- Force of personality

## Building a Hero

Heroes are built from four layers:

1. **Ancestry** -- Your species. Grants a signature trait (free) and a budget of ancestry points to buy additional traits. 13 ancestries: Devil, Dragon Knight, Dwarf, Wode Elf, High Elf, Hakaan, Human, Memonek, Orc, Polder, Revenant, Time Raider.

2. **Background** -- Your upbringing and former job. Composed of:
   - **Culture** (environment, organization, upbringing) -- grants 3 skills + 1 language.
   - **Career** (pre-hero vocation) -- grants 2-3 skills, possibly languages, renown, wealth, project points, and a perk.

3. **Class** -- Your role in combat. 9 classes, each with a unique Heroic Resource that builds during combat and is spent on powerful abilities:
   - **Censor** -- Divine warrior/judge (Wrath)
   - **Conduit** -- Divine healer/caster (Piety)
   - **Elementalist** -- Elemental mage (Essence)
   - **Fury** -- Primal berserker (Ferocity)
   - **Null** -- Psionic anti-magic monk (Discipline)
   - **Shadow** -- Assassin/infiltrator (Insight)
   - **Tactician** -- Battlefield commander (Focus)
   - **Talent** -- Psionic powerhouse (Clarity)
   - **Troubadour** -- Performance-based support (Drama)

4. **Kit** -- Your fighting style and equipment. Determines weapons, armor, and grants a signature weapon ability plus stat bonuses (Stamina, speed, damage, etc.). 21 kits ranging from Shining Armor (max tank) to Martial Artist (fastest skirmisher) to Sniper (long-range damage).

## Combat

- **Alternating activation**: No initiative rolls. Sides alternate taking individual turns (one hero, one monster group, one hero, etc.).
- **On your turn**: One main action + one maneuver + one move action (in any order).
- **Heroic Resource loop**: Resources build each turn and from combat events. Spend them on increasingly powerful heroic abilities.
- **Stamina** (HP): When it hits 0, you are dying but can still act. Death occurs at the negative of your winded value.
- **Conditions**: Bleeding, dazed, frightened, grabbed, prone, restrained, slowed, taunted, weakened.
- **Forced movement**: Push, pull, slide. Slamming into objects/creatures deals extra damage.
- **Flanking**: Two allies on opposite sides of an enemy gain an edge on melee strikes.

## Out-of-Combat Systems

- **Tests**: Power rolls with skills (+2 bonus). Three difficulty levels (easy/medium/hard) shift what each tier means. Montage tests are multi-round collaborative skill challenges.
- **Negotiation**: Structured social encounters. Track NPC Interest (0-5) and Patience (0-5). Appeal to motivations, avoid pitfalls.
- **Downtime Projects**: Crafting and research during respites. Accrue project points toward a goal over multiple rest periods.

## Progression

- **Levels 1-10** across four echelons (1-3, 4-6, 7-9, 10).
- **Victories** earned from encounters convert to XP during respites, which raise your level.
- **Rewards**: Treasures (consumables, trinkets, leveled items, artifacts), Titles (special feats earned from deeds), Renown (fame score that attracts followers), Wealth (abstract purchasing power).

## Setting: Orden and the Timescape

- **Orden** is the prime world. Includes Vasloria (medieval European fantasy analog) and Capital (massive cosmopolitan city of intrigue).
- **The Timescape** is the multiverse -- other planes/manifolds including the Seven Cities of Hell, Axiom (plane of law), the wodes (fey forests), and more.
- **Ajax** is the central villain -- a tyrant who destroyed the Dragon Phalanx and conquered Vasloria.
- **Gods** are real but forbidden from acting directly. They work through saints (once-mortal demigod intermediaries). Churches are consecrated to saints.
- Elder gods include Val (elves), Ord (dwarves), and Kul (orcs), each with distinct domains and saint heroes.
